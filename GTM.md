# GTM.md — Go-to-Market Strategy

## Target User (Specific, Not "Startups")

**Primary persona:** Engineering Manager or VP Engineering at a 5–30 person B2B SaaS company, Series A-stage or bootstrapped, who approves the monthly tool budget. They have a Notion doc somewhere with a list of subscriptions, but they've never seen a consolidated view of their AI tool spend. They suspect they're overpaying — they just haven't had time to verify it.

**What they Google right before needing this tool:**
- "cursor vs copilot which is better 2025"
- "claude team plan pricing comparison"
- "how to reduce AI tool costs startup"
- "github copilot alternatives cheaper"
- "SaaS tool spend audit template"

**Where they hang out online:**
- Reddit: `/r/ExperiencedDevs`, `/r/startups`, `/r/SideProject`
- Hacker News: actively upvote "Show HN" posts about productivity tools
- X/Twitter: following @swyx, @shreyas, @leeerob — the technical founder crowd
- Slack: Indie Hackers community, Supabase community, Vercel community
- Discord: AI-focused developer servers

## First 100 Users in 30 Days (Zero Budget)

### Channel 1: Show HN (Estimated: 30-60 audits)
Post a "Show HN" with a personal story. The key is specificity — not "I built an AI spend auditor" but "I found my 6-person team was paying $228/month for Cursor Business AND GitHub Copilot simultaneously. Here's the free tool I built."

HN rewards authenticity and specificity. The story format (discovery → realization → solution) performs significantly better than a product pitch. Target 3-4 hours of active comment engagement on launch day.

**Draft title:** *"Show HN: I built a free tool to audit your team's AI tool spend (found $228/mo in overlap)"*

### Channel 2: Reddit (Estimated: 20-40 audits)
Post in r/ExperiencedDevs and r/startups on separate days. Same personal story angle. Include the actual audit results — people respond to real numbers. Frame as "I fixed this problem for myself, figured others might benefit."

Key: engage with comments for 2-3 hours after posting. Reddit's algorithm rewards early engagement velocity.

### Channel 3: Cold DMs on X (Estimated: 15-25 audits)
Search Twitter/X for recent tweets about "cursor vs copilot", "AI tool budget", or "SaaS spending". DM 30-50 founders directly: "I saw your tweet about [specific thing]. I built a free tool that gives you an objective answer in 3 minutes — no email required. Mind if I share it?"

The "no email required" line is the hook. It removes the perceived cost.

### Channel 4: Slack Communities (Estimated: 10-20 audits)
Drop the tool in Indie Hackers Slack, Supabase Discord, and similar communities. Frame it as: "I just ran this audit on my own team's stack and found $340/mo — wanted to share in case anyone else is curious about their spend."

Authenticity matters. Don't just drop a link — share your own results first.

### Channel 5: Personal Network (Estimated: 10-15 audits)
Email 10 friends at startups. One well-placed forward in a startup Slack generates 10+ audits. The message: "Hey, built this thing to figure out if we're overpaying for AI tools. Found some stuff. Thought you'd want to know about it."

## Unfair Distribution Advantage

Credex has relationships with AI tool resellers and startup accelerators. This creates a channel that a standalone product couldn't access:

1. **Accelerator welcome packets** — YC, Antler, Pioneer onboarding docs often include "free tools for founders." SpendLens fits perfectly as a free resource. Credex can pitch inclusion to 3-5 accelerators.
2. **TLDR Newsletter sponsorship** — Credex already spends on developer marketing. A single TLDR placement (30k+ developer subscribers) reaches the exact target persona at ~$1,500 — 10x cheaper per lead than most B2B channels.
3. **Reseller relationships** — Companies that already sell Cursor, Claude, or Copilot seats could bundle a free audit offer with their outreach: "Thinking about upgrading? Run a free audit first."

## Retention / Re-engagement Loop

Audits are a one-time event, but AI tool pricing changes quarterly. The re-engagement mechanism:
- 90-day follow-up email: "Tool pricing has changed since your last audit. Run a new one?" (requires storing the audit token + email)
- Credex customers get a "re-audit" link in their monthly invoice email
